GAME: Higurashi When They Cry: Chapter 8 - Matsuribayashi
PLATFORM: PC (Steam Deck)
STARTED ON: 4/29
BEATEN ON: 5/1
TOTAL PLAYTIME: 29 hours

There really is something special about this series.
To pin it down... it'd be a little difficult, but there really is something special.
I started reading Higurashi earnestly two years ago: I finished Chapter 1, Onikakushi, on April 6th, 2023, while sitting on my friend Billy's couch. It really took me a while to get to it.
If any of you are unaware, Higurashi is a sound novel / visual novel where for some reason (I'm still trying really keenly to not spoil anything), the characters go insane and do bad things. It's essentially a murder/mystery narrative where your objective is putting the pieces together before they are explained to you.
Most chapters start with a slice-of-life portion where the overall mystery is not being added to. While you have some good interactions, it really boils down to the characters just being kids playing around.
It almost made me feel guilty. Every single time, it would've just been nice to keep them in stasis like that. "Nothing happened after [that slice of life portion], and they all lived happily ever after". But isn't that more so running away?
Even before reading, knowing there's eight chapters made me dread continuing something like Tatarigoroshi (Week 1 of this year), because I knew those peaceful slice of life days would be over. But was that really worth it? Rip them out of their fun lives and thrust them into the narrative's main plight?
Sure, it sounds silly... I even feel a little silly typing this all out. But it's how I felt!

Even if I've tried my best this whole time to not spoil anything, I'll at least say that Matsuribayashi is a happy ending to the series. There's a bit more Higurashi to go, and it makes me feel guilty. Like I said, it's weird. Who am I to take away from that happy ending? The whole time through eight long chapters, these characters fight to get their fun lives back. These slice-of-life sections are crucial. They show you what they're fighting for. Even when they finally got their reward... how can I just rip it away from them?
Through all of that, it's reassuring to know that there's a happy end at the end of the tunnel, even if it takes eight chapters.

I'm really happy I was bullied into reading Higurashi. It's one of those life-changing works.
There was actually a time, a long, long time back where I would've been in the path of getting into Ryukishi07's works. Back in senior year of high school, I fancied this one nerdy girl whose name I don't even remember anymore.
One day, I saw her in forensics class reading Danganronpa 2 via YouTube video on her phone. To make conversation, since I'd read Danganronpa, I asked what her favorite anime was. She said, with no hesitation, "Umineko When They Cry".
This all happened, probably a decade ago. So my memory's a bit fuzzy. I remember trying to watch the anime. I believe it was either she told me or I saw online to read the visual novel instead. So... during a Steam Sale in very early 2017, I bit the bullet and bought the Question Arcs for Umineko.
Never really got around to reading it, and that one girl and I ended up not really talking all that much after that. But... it's nice that I'm right there, itching to start reading this new mystery.
Most people recommend reading Higurashi before ever even thinking of reading Umineko. If that girl had read Higurashi as well, and preferred Umineko, I wonder if I will too?
Still...
I wish I had given all of this a read back in 2017. That'd be a whole decade of 07th I'd have already done and dusted, with more opportunity to let it stew.
Who knows? Maybe life would've been different. Ha!
