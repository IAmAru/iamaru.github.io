GAME: Song of Saya (PC)
PLATFORM: PC
STARTED ON: 3/3
BEATEN ON: 3/4

I'm not quite sure if I can even say much about the game. It's a really short experience that's definitely not for most people.

Sitting here typing this out, I'm actually wondering if there's anything I can even say without spoiling the entire novel. Don't think I can really recommend it due to its nature and imagery, but if you're interested, the narrative is incredibly engaging.
It's like... Breaking Bad, in a sense. Well, now I don't really wanna spoil Breaking Bad.
There's a shift in the protagonists' morals that makes the entire novel very interesting by about the halfway point.
The novel is really short with a few endings, I believe I clocked in somewhere around 6 or 7 hours.

Funnily enough, I picked this up off Facebook Marketplace. A lady was selling codes for the game for incredibly cheap. I'm assuming she got from a Saya fumo order... who knows, really lol.
This is one of the two digital game purchases I've made this year thus far (although it was technically secondhand).
My first instinct when playing a PC game these days is to try to run it on Steam Deck. Unfortunately it looked like this just wasn't going to work on Deck, unfortunately. The Deck is so nice for visual novels... I missed it when reading this.
I try to attach a picture with every post, but since this was a digital game and I couldn't even play it on the Deck, I'll end off this post without one.
