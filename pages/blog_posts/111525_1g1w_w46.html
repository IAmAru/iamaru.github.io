GAME: Lollipop Chainsaw
PLATFORM: Xbox 360
GENRE: Hack and Slash
STARTED ON: 11/5
BEATEN ON: 11/10
TOTAL PLAYTIME: 5 hours and 50 minutes (I used a stopwatch on my phone lol)

This week, I played the original version of Lollipop Chainsaw on Xbox 360.
It's a decently short game, which got it a lot of annoyed people back when it released, and when the remake, RePOP, released. That's not quite the only thing that got RePOP in controversy but I played the original either way, so RePOP doesn't really matter to me.
Lollipop Chainsaw's biggest strength, in my opinion, is its writing. It's a terrible B-movie on purpose with incredibly out-of-the-blue lines that just want you to get a laugh from the ridiculousness. It's probably James Gunn's best work.
Hearing zombies / rescued students say stupid stuff like, "Girls in Kenya have big butts" or "I regret those three bukkake videos" or "I never thought I'd be saved by someone with such huge tits" always got a laugh out of me. Somehow, they managed to find a way to make the next comment more ridiculous than the last.

The story itself is really entertaining. I think some of the side characters could've been fleshed out just a little more, but they were mostly meant to serve as one-note characters for gags. I think the only one who wasn't more jokey than anything was probably Cordelia.
Rosalind and Juliet's dad were great. Rosalind being a total 2008 middle school scene girl somehow made me feel really nostalgic for the aesthetic (as over-the-top as it is these days).
Juliet and Nick had a funny dynamic. It's a straight man / funny man dynamic, with Nick mostly serving as the straight man.



It also has a great soundtrack, including but not limited to various licensed tracks from artists like Toni Basil, Dragonforce, Joan Jett and the Blackhearts, as well as bangers composed by Akira Yamaoka.

To boot, the game just so happens to be fun- but ultimately brainless- hack-and-slash action. It's not necessarily special or groundbreaking. You go around, kill zombies in a place where you're not allowed to move on until you've killed enough, go to another place, kill more zombies, fight a boss, go to the next stage. No single enemy is too much of a threat or forces you to adapt or change the way you play.
When turning off your brain and just letting the fun writing be the foreground to the gameplay background, it's a lot of fun. It comes back to what I said before- it's a B-movie on purpose. So, it really just feels like hanging out listening to some of your friends' terrible jokes while watching a bad movie together. Somehow, though, the movie's making fun of itself as much as you and your friends are making fun of it.
