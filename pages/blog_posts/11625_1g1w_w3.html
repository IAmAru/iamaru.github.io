GAME: Clock Tower: Rewind
PLATFORM: PlayStation 5
BEATEN ON: 1/9
TOTAL PLAYTIME: 3 hours

Pretty short one this time around.
It's kind of strange to play a horror game like this in January, isn't it? Isn't it something that's best suited for something like Halloween, or October?

I agree! I totally should've played this in October! Of last year! I bought the physical version of this game. Unfortunately Limited Run Games was the publisher. Some of the people who read my posts might not know, but LRG sucks at delivering on time. One of my friends and I joked once that a good insult isn't "I hope you die" or something. A better one is "I hope your favorite game gets a remake published by LRG". That's painful. That's horrifying. And it's completely possible. Beware.
So I received this game in the mail a little bit before Christmas... And I thought, "what a wonderful game to celebrate the holidays... not". I was pretty excited to play this game, though, so. Good a time as any.

I liked the experience, as odd as it can be. I can tell the port was made by people who care a lot about presentation in retro games, especially looking at the display modes. (2x integer scale, "full", etc). Felt like I could drop the PS5 down to 1080p and pipe it to my scaler to have a clean, sharp image.
Didn't get all the endings or trophies, but I would like at some point to double back to do so. It feels like I'm leaving some stuff on the table here by just putting it down after 3 hours.
Some of the new features like "Rewind", where you quite literally rewind time by pressing a button, were... okay? The thing is that a lot of the game is decently slow. If I enter a room to run from the Scissorman / Bobby, and then realize, "dang, that was a bad call, I'll rewind"... the rewind only really takes you back so far, to where you're already making the choice that you want to rewind.
Maybe I didn't understand it. Am I out of touch...? No. It's the game who is wrong. To be honest though, it's refreshing to finally play this one. Ever since that one JonTron video a decade ago, the game's PS1 release has been pretty pricy. Still is.
So many people crown this as part of the beginning to survival horror and it definitely shows. I'd recommend it.
